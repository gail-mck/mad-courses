          How to use this planner

          Building your plan

          • Browse courses in the course catalog on the left side of the screen. Click the subject buttons at the top to jump to a department, or type in the search bar to find a course by name.
          • Open your plan by clicking on the collapsed side bar on the far right side of the screen. Use the vertical bar between the catalog and plan to adjust the size they are displayed.
          • Drag a course card into one of the grade columns on the right to add it to your plan.
          • Move a course by dragging its mini-card from one grade column to another.
          • Remove a course by dragging its mini-card back to the left catalog panel.
          • Click any course card (in the catalog or in your plan) to see its full description and details.

          Tagging courses

          • Click the ⋮ button in the top right of a catalog card to mark a course as ★ Interested, ✓ Taken Previously, or ? Asking About. The tag shows up on the mini-card in your plan too.
          • If you've already taken a prerequisite at a different school, mark it as "Taken Previously" so the planner knows you have it.

          Saving & printing

          • Your plan is saved automatically in your browser. It will still be there if you close the tab and come back.
          • Click Print / Save PDF to download a copy of your plan.

          About the Madeira schedule

          • Blocks are the main academic schedule slots. Most courses are 3 blocks per year. You must fill 18–21 blocks per year (shown by the ✓/✗ counter next to each grade).
          • D-Blocks are daily afternoon activity periods. They are not counted toward your academic block total. You choose one activity per season (Fall, Winter, Spring).
          • Co-Curriculum courses (Student Government, leadership programs, etc.) are separate from your academic schedule and are required each year.
          • Evening courses like Chorus, Orchestra, Stagecraft, and GYLB meet outside of the regular school day. They count as credits toward graduation but do not take up a daytime block.
          • Rotating courses — Linear Algebra, Multivariable Calculus, and Differential Equations — are not offered every year. Their catalog cards show which years they are available.

          Graduation requirements

          Click Distribution Requirements at the bottom of your plan to see a live checklist of how many credits you still need in each subject area. This updates automatically as you add courses.
